PiXL Jargon Buster

PiXL loves its acronyms. Here is what everything actually means in plain English.

KnowIT

A PowerPoint that teaches the key knowledge for a topic, plus a separate question sheet to test recall. Think of it as "teach then test".

GraspIT

Application questions - students must USE their knowledge, not just recall it. Comes in Write On (write on the sheet), Reusable (answer on paper), and Answers (mark scheme) versions.

ThinkIT

Higher-order challenge questions for top students. The hardest of the ITs family.

GetIT

Simplified, scaffolded resources for students aiming at grades 1-2. The most accessible of the ITs family.

DTT

Diagnose-Therapy-Test. A three-step process: students diagnose what they are weak on, work through therapy activities to fix it, then test to check it worked.

Spine

The essential core knowledge students must have for each topic. Think of it as the skeleton of knowledge that everything else builds on. Spine Mats are one-page summaries of this core knowledge.

Knowledge Mat

A detailed, information-dense overview of all key facts for a topic. More comprehensive than a Spine Mat. Edexcel-specific. Split into Part 1 and Part 2.

Therapy & Testing

Intervention resources targeting specific weak areas identified from examiner reports. Ready-made lessons for the topics students consistently lose marks on.

RUAL

Read-Understand-Analyse-Learn. A structured reading and analysis framework used in some PiXL resources.

Strive for Success

Cross-subject resources that help teachers, students, and parents tackle common challenges (motivation, exam anxiety, revision strategies).
